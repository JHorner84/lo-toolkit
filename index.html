Quick Reference — Common Deposit Types & Paper Trail
Deposit Type When to Document Minimum Paper Trail
Payroll direct depositDocument only if irregular or unusually large versus typical pay history.Paystub and explanation. If bonus or variable, verify via VOE or pay history.
Transfer between accountsDocument when deposit meets threshold or source is unclear.Sending and receiving statements showing withdrawal and deposit. Proof of ownership if needed.
Cash depositAlways — high riskDetailed LOE plus hard evidence of cash origin. Confirm investor eligibility.
Gift fundsAlways when used for assets or cash to closeGift letter. Transfer and receipt. Donor ability depends on program and overlay.
Sale of assetAlways when used for assets or cash to closeBill of sale or purchase agreement. Proof of proceeds. Deposit evidence.
Retirement distribution / liquidationAlways when used for assets or cash to closeAccount statement showing distribution or liquidation. Transfer confirmation. Deposit evidence.
Refund or reimbursementDocument if large, unusual, or used for cash to close or reserves.Invoice or receipt, refund confirmation, and deposit evidence.
Unknown ACH / third-party checkAlwaysIdentify source. LOE plus documentation showing origin and transfer.
Crypto proceedsAlways — confirm investor acceptsExchange statements, proof of ownership, liquidation to fiat, and deposit evidence.
Loan advanceAlways — confirm eligibilityAgreement or note, disbursement evidence, and confirm eligibility per program.
Paper Trail Scenarios — Side by Side
Transfer Between Accounts
  • Sending statement showing withdrawal and ending balance
  • Receiving statement showing deposit
  • If ownership is not obvious, document both accounts belong to borrower
Gift Funds
  • Signed gift letter
  • FHA: document donor ability
  • Conventional / Jumbo: gift letter + transfer / receipt (donor ability per overlay)
  • Prefer donor wire to escrow when possible
Sale of Asset
  • Bill of sale or purchase agreement
  • Proof of proceeds (check copy or wire)
  • Deposit evidence on bank statement
Retirement / Stock Liquidation
  • Account statement showing liquidation or distribution
  • Transfer confirmation or check copy
  • Deposit evidence on bank statement
